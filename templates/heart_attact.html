  1. 1. Follow medical instructions

    Strictly adhere to your doctor's recommendations regarding medication, diet, exercise and follow-up visits. Take your medication regularly and as prescribed.

  2. 2. Adopt a healthy lifestyle

    Eat a balanced diet and reduce your intake of salt, saturated fats and sugar. Exercise regularly, but in accordance with your doctor's recommendations.

  3. 3. Control risk factors

    Monitor your blood pressure, cholesterol and blood sugar levels. Avoid smoking and limit alcohol consumption.

  4. 4. Manage stress

    Find relaxation techniques such as meditation, deep breathing or yoga to manage stress. Stress can have a negative impact on your heart.

  5. 5. Watch for warning signs

    Be aware of the signs and symptoms of another heart attack. If you experience chest pain, shortness of breath, dizziness or other unusual symptoms, contact a healthcare professional immediately.

  6. 6. Participate in cardiac rehabilitation programs

    These programs can help you resume progressive physical activity under medical supervision, and provide you with information about adopting a healthy lifestyle.

  7. 7. It is impotant to:

    Maintaining open communication with your doctor and carefully following his or her advice are crucial to improving your heart health and reducing the risk of further complications.
