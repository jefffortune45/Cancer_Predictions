Advice for Diabetes patients

  1. 1. Regular medical check-ups, Diet management

    Consult your doctor regularly for check-ups, examinations, and blood tests.
    Adopt a healthy, balanced diet. Control portion sizes, avoid added sugars and favor high-fiber foods, vegetables, and lean proteins.

  2. 2. Blood sugar control, physical activity

    Check your blood sugar regularly with a glucometer and follow your doctor's recommendations regarding target values.
    Exercise regularly to maintain a healthy weight, improve insulin sensitivity and control blood sugar levels. Consult your physician for recommendations on appropriate physical activity.

  3. 3. Medication, education, and training

    Take your medication as prescribed by your doctor. Take them at the recommended times and doses.
    Learn about diabetes and how to manage your condition. Education sessions can help you understand the disease and adopt healthy lifestyle habits.

  4. 4. Stress management, foot care

    Stress can affect blood sugar levels. Learn relaxation techniques, such as meditation or deep breathing, to manage daily stress.
    Examine your feet regularly for injuries or lesions. Take care of your feet by washing them, drying them thoroughly and wearing comfortable shoes.

  5. 5. Watch for complications, support and community

    Watch for signs of diabetic complications, such as neuropathy, retinopathy or circulation problems. Consult your doctor immediately if you notice any unusual symptoms.
    Seek out support groups or online communities to share your experiences, receive support and exchange advice with others living with diabetes.

    Working closely with your medical team is essential to managing diabetes effectively and reducing the risk of long-term complications.
