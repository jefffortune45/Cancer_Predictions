Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is a malignant disease that develops in the cells of the breast. It can take various forms, including ductal and lobular carcinomas. It is one of the most common diseases in women, although men can also be affected. Early detection is essential, and can be achieved by methods such as breast self-examination and regular mammograms. Common symptoms of breast cancer include nodules in the breasts, changes in breast shape or size, pain and discharge from the nipples. Treatment varies according to the stage of the cancer, but may include surgery, chemotherapy, radiotherapy and other modalities. Breast cancer prevention involves raising awareness, reducing risk factors such as excessive alcohol consumption and obesity, and promoting healthy lifestyles. Ongoing research is essential to improve treatment and quality of life for those affected by this disease.

Skin Cancer

Skin cancer, also known as cutaneous cancer, is a form of cancer that develops in the cells of the skin. It can take different forms, including basal cell carcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma and melanoma. Skin cancer is usually caused by excessive exposure to the sun's ultraviolet (UV) rays or to artificial tanning beds. Early detection is crucial to effective treatment, and regular monitoring for changes in moles, spots or skin lesions is essential. Sun protection and safety practices, such as using sunscreen and avoiding excessive sun exposure, play an essential role in preventing skin cancer.

Heart Attack

Myocardial infarction, commonly known as heart attack, is a serious condition that occurs when blood flow to the heart muscle is blocked or severely reduced, often due to the build-up of atheromatous plaque in the coronary arteries. This can lead to life-threatening damage to the heart muscle. The most common symptoms are intense chest pain, shortness of breath, excessive sweating and pain in the arms, neck or jaw. Immediate medical intervention is crucial to minimize damage to the heart muscle and improve chances of survival.

Take Precautions to Prevent Cancer

Eat a diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Avoid excess saturated fat, sugar, and salt.

Smoking is one of the leading causes of many cancers, including lung cancer. If you smoke, seriously consider quitting, and avoid exposure to second-hand smoke.

Limit sun exposure, especially during peak hours. Use sunscreen with a high sun protection factor and wear protective clothing.
Engage in Regular Physical Activity Regular exercise helps maintain a healthy weight and strengthen the immune system, reducing the risk of certain types of cancer.
